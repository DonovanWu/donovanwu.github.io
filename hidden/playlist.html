You've found my playlist!

Once a colleague of mine joked that we could play music out loud in the office the day before Chinese New Year, since nobody would be at the office. And our boss replied by asking what music we would play. So, here's a list of music I would totally turn the volume up to the max for when I am the only person still at work! And no, it doesn't include If You Do Do. XD

A bit about my taste: my taste is probably different from most people around me. It's not just that I rarely listen to Chinese songs. You'll notice that there's barely any love songs in the list below. But really, it's not that I avoid "love songs", but rather, I generally wouldn't like a song too much if it's about "yearning". And the songs I like usually fall into one of the two cases below, and sometimes it's both:

  1. It's about personal struggles
  2. It's connected to an experience

If a song I like is the theme song of an anime or a game that I really liked, it falls into the second case. Otherwise, it tells a story about someone's personal struggle. Well... I guess different people have different definitions for "personal struggle", though. For example, some people may consider a person's pursuit of external things such as money, fame, and "success", to be personal struggles, because, well, you must work very hard to obtain these. But, to me, those are insignificant struggles. What I consider to be "personal struggles" are much more unconquerable things, and in the end, you might not win at all. And perhaps that says something about me.

Note: the playlist is shuffled every time you enter this page, since I don't want the order to be misconstrued as my rating for how much I love each song.

  • この手で (With These Hands)

    Case: Personal struggle

    Comments:

    For those who already know this song... I see, you're a man of culture as well. XD

    If you read into the lyrics, though, it's actually insanely good in that in a few lines of words, it has constructed an incredibly complex and multi-layered character. The female protagonist (I assume it's a female protagonist) clearly had some pretty traumatic past experience, which, despite her best effort, she couldn't get over (あの頃の記憶が今も / 傷迹になったまま 消せなくて). But it would look like she was not completely innocent herself, either. She knew how to do bad things and, driven by the ruthless environment she lived in, had also done bad things (or at least that's my interpretation for 罪で汚れた傷口に). All she could do to numb the pain was hurting herself even further, sometimes in a quite literal sense (爪をたて新しい 痛みでごまかしてる). However, she still believed in the good in the world, and wanted to make a leap of faith to pursue it (運命に身を任せ 目を闭じる). Nevertheless, she had already realized the meaninglessness of it all (なのに ダメ わたし「意味」まだ探してる). But more than anything, she hated that she couldn't break this cycle of tragedy (and perhaps she herself was falling down further, or perhaps she had unintentionally dragged someone else into her madness), so she made up her mind to put an end to this at all cost (この手で 今この手で 断ち切って / 悲しみの連鎖に 終止符をうって), even if the path ahead was clearly martyrdom (涙が この涙が 渇いたら / もう一度世界に いつか見たあの光を).

    This song has some of the most intense personal struggle I've seen depicted (in songs, to say the least). The female protagonist is a victim, is strong, is a conflicting person, and ultimately, is determined. Hardly any songs can rival the quality of this one in terms of lyrics.

    There are actually two versions of this song, same lyrics but performed by two different singers. This one is performed by Yakushi Ruri (薬師るり), which is the superior version in my opinion. Her voice has the just right amount of toughness in it, which perfectly illustrates how the female protagonist is a strong girl, while she's also strengthless in face of the things she's set out to do.

  • Call of Silence

    Case: Personal struggle + Experience

    Comments:

    Alright, this song actually has lyrics, but I chose a lyricless version, just piano solo. This specific cover more properly conveyed the powerful feeling of this piece, I think.

    This is the music that played during the backstory of Ymir in the anime Attack on Titan. Not the Founding Titan Ymir, the Jaw Titan Ymir. I know it's confusing to those who haven't watched the anime (LOL). Ymir's backstory is that she has died... turned into a titan, technically. So her human consciousness has died, but she lived as a titan for a few hundred years. Then, by pure coincidence, she managed to devour the human who had Jaw Titan power, and got to live as a human once again. When she woke up, she found herself lying naked in the desert. And then, she saw the "path" under the night sky, like the Milky Way, as she bursted out laughing, at her fate, probably. It was when she decided to live the rest of her life authentically. So she went on to be a lowlife.

    And it was an incredibly powerful imagery, at least to me, Ymir laughing at the sky with no one around while being naked and all. It's sort of a symbolism, as in some languages, the word "naked" can mean being "true" or "authentic". And that is reflected in Ymir's decision on how she starts her new life. To live authentically is easy to say, but it is even easier for people to give it up. And there's no shortage of excuse as to why you aren't trying to pursue your dreams. Easily there could be some kind of an oppressor figure to be blamed on, be it your boss, your parents, the social environment, or sometimes you could feel that your upbringing has hindered you. Not to downplay these obstacles, but to live one's life authentically usually means sorting out your priorities and making sacrifices, too. If you want everything, that's just greed. And to realize this and have the resolve to make such sacrifices is why Ymir's story feels so powerful to me. In her case, she went back to the kind of life she felt lucky to have escaped from. The anime may have kind of glossed over it, but you could feel the manga author's intention there. It was no easy decision for Ymir.

    And the story became doubly powerful as Ymir went on to influence Historia, as she saw a lot of herself in Historia, and sort of have romantic feelings for her. And eventually, Historia was also able to be true to herself, though her choice was different. While Ymir wanted to live sort of like a hermit, Historia agreed to be installed as the Queen to end the political chaos in Paradis Island, but you could feel it was her authentic choice, that she wanted to take the responsibility. This comes with great sacrifice, too, noticeably, a lot of her personal freedom. That is something many people never think of, but Attack on Titan did a fairly good job making this point.

    Pop quiz: I've always wanted to see Ymir get married to Queen Historia. Sadly, that didn't happen in Attack on Titan. But suppose we're writing a fanfiction about it. How should we call Ymir then? Queen Ymir? Queen's queen? Queen's wife?

    ... (Not letting you see the answer too quickly. Please think about it a little bit. LOL)

    The answer is actually "Princess Ymir", though such a marriage has never happened in the real world. What did happen in the real world was Queen Elizabeth and Prince Philip. So by deduction, if a Queen, as the head of a state, has a wife instead, her wife's title should be "princess".

She's totally feeling like she's the one who in fact has a say between she and him, as she's making this nice guy giving in to... let's just say, his more primitive instincts. But this turned around in the second verse, as the girl starts to lose control, not because the guy starts to take over, but rather, she's losing control of herself. This is because her mental state worsened, likely resulting in her demanding more of such "thrills" to distract herself from everything else in life that makes her suffer. She finds herself becoming more and more reliant on the boy, as he's likely the only person she could truly trust. He has become her hero. And hence the lyrics goes "きっと 滅びてはじまる / 救いの手伸ばして". However, their relationship isn't perfect, either. This whole thing is actually less conseual on the boy's side. He's not comfortable with hurting others, but he couldn't bring himself to just leave her, either. And the girl is very desperate to have him stay by her side. However, in Venus in Furs, the male protagonist was eventually disappointed seeing that his partner was still so readily accepting her traditional gender role despite his effort to make her see an alternative way.

  • バケモノと呼ばれて (lit. Call Me a Monster)

    Case: Experience

    Comments:

    This is the ending song of the anime Talentless Nana, or 「無能なナナ」. Well... The translation can be a bit misleading, since 「無能」 can also mean "without ability" or "without power". And if you have watched the show, you know that's pretty much all the anime is about. Alright, I can't talk about it without some spoilers, so... proceed with your own discretion. (LOL)

    So, for the most part of the first episode, you would have thought that it's a very average superpower fighting anime. A bunch of teenagers with all sorts of superpower were brought to a school to hone their skills. Among them was the female protagonist Nana, who claimed that her superpower was mind reading. And the "male protagonist", who, by the end of the episode, would reveal that his power was clearly inspired by Kamijou Touma from Toaru Majutsu no Index (LOL). Except by the end of the episode you find out that Nana is a regular human without power (hence "talentless"), and is sent to assassinate humans with superpower, and the "male protagonist" became her first victim. Yea... So, it was pretty mind-blowing. It's a fun show to watch, seeing how the female protagonist beat superpower with sheer intelligence. Sadly, the pace of the show slowed down drastically towards the second half of the show, and the story is not finished. They were expecting a Season 2 that never materialized. To be fair, that's a problem with many anime nowadays.

    I find myself associating an anime with its ending song more than its opening song. I don't know exactly why but I think opening songs nowadays are kind of generic. They are clearly made to excite the audience a bit before the show begins. But ending songs can be relatively more freeform and consequently more expressive.

    One more thing. There was one part in this song I found kind of jarring at first, which is where the line 「もうちょっとだけ」 was repeated three times, which literally translates to "just a little more". But what I realized is that, if you put it in the context, this line should actually translate to "just give it one more chance", and that part was saying: "If you just say 'just give it another chance; just give it another chance; just give it another chance', perhaps I'll forgive this world." ...which is fitting, and it makes sense to be repeated three times.

So, no, for parallel lines to cross, you'll at least have to go out of the observable universe. (LOL)
